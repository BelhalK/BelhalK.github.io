    I am currently a Ph.D. student in Machine Learning at Ecole Polytechnique (CMAP), under the supervision of Marc Lavielle and Eric Moulines. I am a member of the XPOP team, a joint team between INRIA and CMAP focussing on statistical modelling for life sciences. My research interests lie in the fields of high dimensional statistics and machine learning. My current work focuses on the acceleration and optimization of training algorithms for large datasets. I am, in particular, working on Maximum Likelihood estimation algorithms for mixed effects models. Prior that, I've visited the Massachusetts Institute of Technology where I've spent most of my time at the Probabilistic Computing Project working on MCMC methods.
    I've been using data science and machine learning techniques in various domains such as :

    Social Sciences: Telecom ParisTech - Behaviours towards private data disclosure [PDF]
    Economics: Agoranov - Importance of Startups in job creation
    Education: MIT - US College Scorecard [PDF]
    Growth & Development: Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation - HBGDki [PDF] and [Slides]

    At the moment, I am mainly interested in Machine Learning and Computational Social Science.
