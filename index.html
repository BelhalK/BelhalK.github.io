Belhal Karimi

Ph.D. Candidate
Ecole Polytechnique & INRIA
CMAP, XPOP

I have recently defended my Ph.D. thesis at Ecole Polytechnique (CMAP), under the supervision of Marc Lavielle and Eric Moulines. From October 2016 to September 2019 I was a member of the XPOP team, a joint team between INRIA and CMAP focussing on statistical modelling for life sciences. My research interests lie in the fields of high dimensional statistics and machine learning. My current work focuses on the acceleration and optimization of training algorithms for latent data models. Prior that, I've visited the Massachusetts Institute of Technology where I've spent most of my time at the Probabilistic Computing Project working on MCMC methods.

Research interests

My work lies in the intersection of machine learning, statistics and optimization.
I've been using data science and machine learning techniques in various domains such as :

Healthcare: Xpop & Lixoft - Pharmacokinetics [Slides]
Social Sciences: Telecom ParisTech - Behaviours towards private data disclosure [PDF]
Economics: Agoranov - Importance of Startups in job creation
Education: MIT - US College Scorecard [PDF]
Growth & Development: Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation - HBGDki [PDF], [Slides] and [Poster]
